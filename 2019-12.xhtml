Maura Schumacher had asked about bird cherries in Kasilof. I responded:

Dear Maura, In that Kasilof beach population there are a number of individuals (maybe 10 or more?), some of them large and reproducing. There is no standing water, but there is a little seep-fed stream. I do not think the stream plays much of a role in dispersal at this site; I think dispersal here is mainly by birds. This is in on Borough land, but it is under a lease and you will need to contact the lessee. I think the lessee had previously granted permission for the bird cherries to be treated, but then nothing was done. The source of this population is likely a couple of large bird cherry trees on private land above the wetland. Also, see the observation records at the URI below. https://www.inaturalist.org/observations?nelat=60.84462282891534&nelng=-148.58626158007814&place_id=any&swlat=60.100162353960734&swlng=-151.7310980058594&taxon_id=143793 The record at Gwin's Lodge would be worth checking out (URI below) to determine whether or not these are escaping. The worst place to have birch cherries on the Kenai Peninsula would be upstream on any of long streams/rivers. We would not want bird cherries getting into the Kenai River in Cooper Landing. https://www.inaturalist.org/observations/3190422 Also, I saw small feral bird cherries in a vacant lot off of Lovers Lane in Soldotna near 60.478508°N, 151.075664°W. -Matt

Reading up on ordination/classification methods (see https://ourcodingclub.github.io/2018/05/04/ordination.html and vegan vegdist function documentation), it looks like the Jaccard or Raup-Crick indices would be more appropriate for presence/absence data.

From https://ourcodingclub.github.io/2017/03/21/data-clustering.html#Distance:

When working with large presence/absence datasets, it is good practice to remove “uniques”. “Uniques” are species that only have one recorded presence in only one observation/sample. The reason behind this is that such species will only bring noise (somewhat random, hard to explain variation) to the analysis and will only blur the patterns we get. Later on, for the sake of practice, we’ll compare the results we’ll get with the full presence and absence matrix with the results we’ll get with the same matrix without any “Uniques”.

I will try removing uniques.
