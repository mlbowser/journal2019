Wednesday, July 17

  • time sheet
  • 2016 Slikok project identifications.

I continued with identifications from the Slikok project, finishing the Diptera through Hemiptera: Cicadellidae.

One interesting record was Idiocerus elegans Flor, 1861, with 27 and 44 reads from KNWR:Ento:10895 and KNWR:Ento:10896, respectively. This may be a new record for Alaska and North America. Hosts of genus Idiocerus are willows and poplars.

Monday, July 22

I spent most of the day editing a list of exotic species from Alaska for Annie Simpson at USGS.

Late in the day I found that I had received COI sequences from recent Lifescanner vials that I had sent in. The interesting record was UAM:Ento:377285, which I had tentatively identified as Bimastos parvus on April 15. The COI sequence we obtained from this specimen was distant (90.52% p-dist similarity) from sequences identified as B. parvus on BOLD and was 99.55% similar to sequences from BOLD BIN BOLD:AAF9647, a BIN from Canada where specimens were only identified as Lumbricidae.
