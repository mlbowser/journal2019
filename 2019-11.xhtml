Saturday, November 2

When looking up documentation of FWSpecies, I stumpled across fwspp, an R package for looking up species occurrences on Refuges.

I started going through the list of species that need to be added to FWSpecies.

Baryphyma longitarsum is now Micrargus longitarsus. I updated Arctos record KNWR:Ento:6464 and added Micrargus longitarsus to the Refuge's species list in FWSpecies.

Kaestneria anceps was on the list to add, but I found no record of this species from the Kenai National Wildlife Refuge. The accepted name of this species is now Kaestneria pullata. There are multiple records of this species from the Kenai Peninsula on GBIF. I removed this from the list for now.

Monday, November 4

I updated records on Arctos identified as Kaestneria anceps to Kaestneria pullata. I added this species to Kenai NWR's list as "Probably Present" with the note, "Kaestneria pullata is known from Kenai, Seward, and Homer, and very likely occurs on Kenai National Wildlife Refuge, but there have been no specimens identified from the Refuge." I am not sure how "Probably Present" records show up on the checklist (or not). I checked: this record does now show up in our checklist as "Probably Present." The history notes are not included FWSpecies' export, though.

I added Kaestneria rufula to the Kenai NWR's checklist based on Slikok project record UAMObs:Ento:240442.

I removed Theridion montanum from the list of additions. It is now a synonym of Canalidion montanum, which I added to the Refuge's list in FWSpecies.
