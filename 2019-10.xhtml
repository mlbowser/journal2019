Tuesday, October 22

I worked on composing my Refuge Notebook article on Operophtera bruceata.

I just found out that Operophtera specimens from Alaska formerly identified as Operophtera bruceata are now Operophtera occidentalis. See discussion at https://www.inaturalist.org/observations/18618236. BOLD specimen GNAU149-10 from Sunrise is currently identified on BOLD as Operophtera bruceata in BIN BOLD:AAA2999. BOLD specimen GNAU152-10, also from Sunrise, is identified as Operophtera occidentalis and was placed in BIN BOLD:AAA3000. All four Alaskan specimens of Operophtera from Alaska are from Sunrise. Of these, two are O. bruceata and two are O. occidentalis, so both are present on the Kenai Peninsula.

I found that Pohl et al. (2018) rasied O. bruceata occidentalis back up to a full species based on the data from Gwiazdowski et al. (2013). The best key separating these appears to be Troubridge and Fitzpatrick (1993):

  • Abdomen brown to golden-brown. Two color forms: dark form with forewing dorsal surface lines faint but recognizable; light form with forewing dorsal surface lines distinct and dark, almost fused to form dark bands; light bands within cell M3 darker than other cells. Distributed west of the Cascade Mts and Coast Mts . . . O. bruceata occidentalis
  • Abdomen brown. Costa of forewing ventral surface brown. Wing colour grey west and brown east of Rocky Mts. Forewing dorsal surface lines distinct but pale, not fused to form dark bands; light bands within cell M3 no darker than within other cells. Occurs east of the Cascade and Coast Mts . . . O. bruceata bruceata.

I photographed and collected one specimen on the side of the headquarters building that looks to me more like O. occidentalis than O. bruceata (iNaturalist: 34758287). I took the left midleg to be sequenced via LifeScanner (vial BOLD-CU4) and pinned and spread the specimen (KNWR:Ento:11340).

Todd photographed an individual from Headquarters that really looks like good O. occidentalis (iNaturalist: 34757881).
