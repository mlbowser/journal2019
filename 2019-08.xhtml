I wrote John:

I cannot distinguish whether the Myriophyllum specimens you gave me are Myriophyllum verticillatum or Myriophyllum sibiricum. I checked the most complete and up-to-date treatment (URI below). The number of leaflets is not a reliable character and overlaps greatly between these two species. http://floranorthamerica.org/files/Haloragaceae.provisional.Gal_.pdf The best characters for separating these two species are the bracts on the inflorescences and the winter turions. The turions on these specimens are only just starting to form. As the water temperature cools this fall the turions should form and we could collect these. The turions of M. verticillatum are club-shaped; the turions of M. sibiricum are cylindrical. See the image at the URI below. Right now there may still be some flowering stems to be found emerging from the water, especially in shallower water. http://floranordica.org/Review/-ill-html-filer/ill_Myriophyllum-tur.html

Tuesday, August 27

I need to make arrangements for shipping out and processing Tracy's samples.

For her fungal analyses from last year MrDNA used GATGAAGAACGYAGYRAA for the forward primer and CTNTTVCCKCTTCACTCG for the reverse primer. The forward primer is ITS3_KYO2 from Toju et al. (2012).

The reverse primer used by MrDNA was CTNTTVCCKCTTCACTCG, which I could not find an exact match for. It is almost identical to the primer ITS4_KYO3 (CTBTTVCCKCTTCACTCG) from Toju et al. (2012).

We had requested ITS3F/ITS4R (ITS3F: GCATCGATGAAGAACGCAGC, ITS4R: TCCTCCGCTTATTGATATGC

The arthropod primers used by Tracy for last year's samples were GGWACWGGWTGAACWGTWTAYCCYCC (forward) and TAIACYTCIGGRTGICCRAARAAYCA (reverse). I see that the reverse primer is slightly different than what we requested (mlCOI2-jgHCO2198, TANACYTCNGGRTGNCCRAARAAYCA) in that the primer used has an inosine at position three instead of an N (any nucleotide) mlCOI2-jgHCO2198 was used here: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsos.160635.

I wrote RTL:

I will be preparing to send our first sets of samples of soil fungi and terrestrial arthropods out to RTL next week for metabarcoding.

These particular samples are from the second year of a multi-year project. Even though the metabarcoding of samples was performed by a different lab last year, I would like to use the same primer sets if possible to maintain some consistency.

For soil fungi the other lab used the primers below.

Forward: GATGAAGAACGYAGYRAA
Reverse: CTNTTVCCKCTTCACTCG

The forward primer is ITS3_KYO2 from Toju et al. (2012); the reverse primer is almost identical to the primer ITS4_KYO3 (CTBTTVCCKCTTCACTCG) from Toju et al. (2012).

I was surprised by this primer selection because we had asked for the standard ITS3F/ITS4R primer pair.

For arthropods the other lab used the primers below.

Forward: GGWACWGGWTGAACWGTWTAYCCYCC
Reverse: TAIACYTCIGGRTGICCRAARAAYCA

This is basically the mlCOI2/jgHCO2198 primer pair except that the reverse primer varies by one nucleotide at position 3 (standard jgHCO2198: TANACYTCNGGRTGNCCRAARAAYCA). I think it would be fine to use either of these reverse primers.

Please let me know if these primers are available. I also welcome your input on primer selection.

Sincerely,

Matt

Thursday, August 29

I need to decide on the appropriate microbial diversity assay for LTEMP/Slikok soil samples from this year. I downloaded RTL Genomics' assay list. Their default for ITS is ITS3F GCATCGATGAAGAACGCAGC ITS4R TCCTCCGCTTATTGATATGC. Looking at the Toju et al. (2012) article, it does appear that the primers they designed have better coverage.

The ITS86F/ITS4 primer pair from Op De Beeck et al. (2014) looks good. The ITS3mix-ITS4ngs cocktail of Tedersoo et al (2015) looks even better. These are ITS3mix: CANCGATGAAGAACGYRG and ITS4ngs: TCCTSCGCTTATTGATATGC.

There is a comparison of fungal primers at https://unite.ut.ee/primers.php.

I think I will go for ITS3mix/ITS4ngs for the 2019 LTEMP/Slikok samples.
