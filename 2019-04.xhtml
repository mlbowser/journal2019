Friday, April 5

I worked on cleaning up the STDP HTS data in preparation for merging the morphology-based and molecular ID datasets (R script). I also started scrutinizing unidentified sequences and running these individually on BOLD and Blastn. BOLD was down for a while, though.

The most noteworthy find was an apparently solid identification of Placusa incompleta (Coleoptera: Staphylinidae), a Palearctic species adventive in North America and now present on both coasts from Washington and British Columbia to Quebec. This looks like a new record for AK. We picked it up in Anchorage in a trap where bycatch had not been removed.
