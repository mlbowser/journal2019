Wednesday, September 25

John and I were flown out to Vogel Lake cabin to survey for elodea. We surveyed 51 points in Vogel Lake and the small lake north of Vogel Lake, finding diverse aquatic vegetation, but no elodea.

We walked up to Sandpiper Lake, where Rob Massengill had reported elodea. We threw the rake from shore a little, but we saw no elodea there.

John throwing the rake in Vogel Lake.

Back at the cabin I could see that the soils were obiously worm-worked, with no leafy litter except for what had fallen recently. Using mustard powder and water I collected earthworms, some of which looked like Lumbricus terrestris. The adults were reluctant to come up, though.

I dug one soil pit behind the cabin. I was surprised that there was a thick, roughly 2-3 cm layer of what looked and felt like volcanic ash on the top of the mineral soil just below the leaf litter. Below this was loess.
