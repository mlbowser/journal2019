Monday, March 25

I entered data for and examined series KNWR:Ento:11316, a series of stoneflies from the Kasilof River that I had collected on Thursday. I obtained Nebeker and Gaufin (1965, 1967) and compared my specimens to these illustrations. My specimens are Utacapnia columbiana (Claassen, 1924), the same species I had previously collected from the Soldotna area. Nebeker and Gaufin (1965) listed a specimen record from Deep Creek taken in 1956.

There are currently no sequences of Utacapnia combiana from Alaska on BOLD, so I put one male in lifescanner vial BOLD-P00 → KNWR:Ento:11317.

Specimen KNWR:Ento:11317, habitus.

Specimen KNWR:Ento:11317, genitalia.

Specimen KNWR:Ento:8873 is also Utacapnia columbiana.

I cloned these records into the UAM:Ento collection so that I can donate them to UAM. I am trying to downsize our collection.

Regarding Utacapnia, I was surprised that, according to Stewart (1997), "there have been no published accounts of biology of any species in this genus." I wonder if that has changed.

Thursday, March 28

I worked at home a little in the morning, updating the Biology News page on our website.

I continued with the STDP analysis, looking at assigned taxonomy. See R script, another R script assembling the identifications, and tree making I/O using QIIME and iTOL.

I checked the pan traps I had put out yesterday. All they had collected was one large fly in the dish baited with vinegar and one immature delphacid in the dish baited with molasses and yeast. I collected the fly and let the live delphacid go. I did not end up retaining the fly specimen.
